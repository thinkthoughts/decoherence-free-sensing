Decoherence-Free Subspaces Specify Robust Quantum Sensing

Constraining quantum evolution to a decoherence-free subspace removes common-mode noise while preserving differential phase information, enabling scalable entanglement-enhanced sensing.

PaperPRXRepo

Core claim

Robust quantum sensing is specified by restricting evolution to a decoherence-free subspace. Within that constraint, entangled states preserve sensitivity to differential signals while suppressing dominant common-mode noise.

Observe

Restricting dynamics to a decoherence-free subspace allows differential phase accumulation without sensitivity to common-mode phase noise.

Classical

Removing common-mode noise leaves only the differential signal. Practical cavity protocols enable scalable sensing beyond the Standard Quantum Limit.

Engineering statement

Decoherence-free subspaces specify admissible quantum sensor designs: constrain dynamics to reject shared noise first, then engineer entanglement that maximizes sensitivity to the remaining differential signal.
